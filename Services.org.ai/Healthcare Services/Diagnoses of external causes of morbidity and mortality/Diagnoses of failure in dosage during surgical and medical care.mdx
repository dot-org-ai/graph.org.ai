# Diagnoses of failure in dosage during surgical and medical care

**UNSPSC Code**: 85446800
**Family**: [Diagnoses of external causes of morbidity and mortality](Diagnoses of external causes of morbidity and mortality.mdx)
**Segment**: [Healthcare Services](../Healthcare Services.mdx)

In this classification, this covers the group of activities that yields a diagnosis of failure in dosage during surgical and medical care. Failure in dosage during surgical and medical care is listed In The World Health Organization's International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD-10) as a four-character category under code Y63. ICD-10 also has The following Failure in dosage during surgical and medical care children in its four-character category: Y63.0 (Excessive amount of blood or other fluid given during transfusion or infusion); Y63.1 (Incorrect dilution of fluid used during infusion); Y63.2 (Overdose of radiation given during therapy); Y63.3 (Inadvertent exposure of patient to radiation during medical care); Y63.4 (Failure in dosage in electroshock or insulin-shock therapy); Y63.5 (Inappropriate temperature in local application and packing); Y63.6 (Nonadministration of necessary drug, medicament or biological substance); Y63.8 (Failure in dosage during other surgical and medical care); Y63.9 (Failure in dosage during unspecified surgical and medical care)

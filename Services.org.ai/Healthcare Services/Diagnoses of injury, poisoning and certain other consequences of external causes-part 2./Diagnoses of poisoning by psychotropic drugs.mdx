# Diagnoses of poisoning by psychotropic drugs

**UNSPSC Code**: 85433500
**Family**: [Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2.](Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2..mdx)
**Segment**: [Healthcare Services](../Healthcare Services.mdx)

In this classification, this covers the group of activities that yields a diagnosis of poisoning by psychotropic drugs, not elsewhere classified. Poisoning by psychotropic drugs, not elsewhere classified is listed In The World Health Organization's International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD-10) as a four-character category under code T43. ICD-10 also has The following Poisoning by psychotropic drugs, not elsewhere classified children in its four-character category: T43.0 (Poisoning: Tricyclic and tetracyclic antidepressants ); T43.1 (Poisoning: Monoamine-oxidase-inhibitor antidepressants ); T43.2 (Poisoning: Other and unspecified antidepressants ); T43.3 (Poisoning: Phenothiazine antipsychotics and neuroleptics ); T43.4 (Poisoning: Butyrophenone and thioxanthene neuroleptics ); T43.5 (Poisoning: Other and unspecified antipsychotics and neuroleptics ); T43.6 (Poisoning: Psychostimulants with abuse potential ); T43.8 (Poisoning: Other psychotropic drugs, not elsewhere classified ); T43.9 (Poisoning: Psychotropic drug, unspecified )

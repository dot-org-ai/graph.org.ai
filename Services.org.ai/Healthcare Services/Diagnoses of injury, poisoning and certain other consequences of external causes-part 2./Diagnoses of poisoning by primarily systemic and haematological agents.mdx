# Diagnoses of poisoning by primarily systemic and haematological agents

**UNSPSC Code**: 85433700
**Family**: [Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2.](Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2..mdx)
**Segment**: [Healthcare Services](../Healthcare Services.mdx)

In this classification, this covers the group of activities that yields a diagnosis of poisoning by primarily systemic and haematological agents, not elsewhere classified. Poisoning by primarily systemic and haematological agents, not elsewhere classified is listed In The World Health Organization's International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD-10) as a four-character category under code T45. ICD-10 also has The following Poisoning by primarily systemic and haematological agents, not elsewhere classified children in its four-character category: T45.0 (Poisoning: Antiallergic and antiemetic drugs ); T45.1 (Poisoning: Antineoplastic and immunosuppressive drugs ); T45.2 (Poisoning: Vitamins, not elsewhere classified ); T45.3 (Poisoning: Enzymes, not elsewhere classified ); T45.4 (Poisoning: Iron and its compounds ); T45.5 (Poisoning: Anticoagulants ); T45.6 (Poisoning: Fibrinolysis-affecting drugs ); T45.7 (Poisoning: Anticoagulant antagonists, vitamin K and other coagulants ); T45.8 (Poisoning: Other primarily systemic and haematological agents ); T45.9 (Poisoning: Primarily systemic and haematological agent, unspecified )

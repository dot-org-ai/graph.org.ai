# Diagnoses of poisoning by anaesthetics and therapeutic gases

**UNSPSC Code**: 85433300
**Family**: [Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2.](Diagnoses of injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes-part 2..mdx)
**Segment**: [Healthcare Services](../Healthcare Services.mdx)

In this classification, this covers the group of activities that yields a diagnosis of poisoning by anaesthetics and therapeutic gases. Poisoning by anaesthetics and therapeutic gases is listed In The World Health Organization's International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems (ICD-10) as a four-character category under code T41. ICD-10 also has The following Poisoning by anaesthetics and therapeutic gases children in its four-character category: T41.0 (Poisoning: Inhaled anaesthetics ); T41.1 (Poisoning: Intravenous anaesthetics ); T41.2 (Poisoning: Other and unspecified general anaesthetics ); T41.3 (Poisoning: Local anaesthetics ); T41.4 (Poisoning: Anaesthetic, unspecified ); T41.5 (Poisoning: Therapeutic gases )

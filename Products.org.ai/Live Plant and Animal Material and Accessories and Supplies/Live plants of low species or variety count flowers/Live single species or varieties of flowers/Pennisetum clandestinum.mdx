Pennisetum clandestinum is a tropical perennial species of Poaceae with several common names, kikuyo, quicuyo, coarse grass, African grass, which comes from the East African region, home to the Kĩkũyũ nation. It has fast growth and aggressiveness, which is why it is categorized as a weed in some regions1. However, it is also a popular turfgrass in Australia and South Africa due to its cheapness and tolerance to drought. It is also widely used as a livestock pasture, with low quality, but very rich in protein.

# Integrated services digital network ISDN access devices

**UNSPSC Code**: 43222627
**Class**: [Network service equipment](Network service equipment.mdx)
**Family**: [Data Voice or Multimedia Network Equipment or Platforms and Accessories](../Data Voice or Multimedia Network Equipment or Platforms and Accessories.mdx)
**Segment**: [Information Technology Broadcasting and Telecommunications](../../Information Technology Broadcasting and Telecommunications.mdx)

A client device providing ISDN digital transmission over ordinary telephone copper wire as well as over other media. ISDN requires adapters at both ends of the transmission so your access provider also needs an ISDN adapter. Equipment used to adapt Integrated Services Digital Network (ISDN) Basic Rate Interface (BRI) channels to existing terminal equipment standards such as EIA-232 and V.35. A Terminal Adaptor is typically packaged like a modem, either as a stand-alone unit or as an interface card that plugs into a computer or other communications equipment (such as a router or PBX).A Terminal Adaptor does not interoperate with a modem; it replaces it.

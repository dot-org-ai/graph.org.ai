# Surety bonds

**UNSPSC Code**: 64122400
**Family**: [Insurable interest contracts](Insurable interest contracts.mdx)
**Segment**: [Financial Instruments, Products, Contracts and Agreements](../Financial Instruments, Products, Contracts and Agreements.mdx)

Surety bond or surety is a promise by a surety or guarantor to pay one party (the obligee) a certain amount if a second party (the principal) fails to meet some obligation, such as fulfilling the terms of a commercial contract. The surety bond protects the obligee against losses resulting from the principal's failure to meet the obligation.

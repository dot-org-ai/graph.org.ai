# Set and Exhibit Designers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 27-1027.00

Design special exhibits and sets for film, video, television, and theater productions. May study scripts, confer with directors, and conduct research to determine appropriate architectural styles.

## Tasks

- [Develop set designs, based on evaluation of scripts, budgets, research information, and available locations.](Tasks/9289.mdx)
- [Prepare rough drafts and scale working drawings of sets, including floor plans, scenery, and properties to be constructed.](Tasks/9286.mdx)
- [Prepare preliminary renderings of proposed exhibits, including detailed construction, layout, and material specifications, and diagrams relating to aspects such as special effects or lighting.](Tasks/9294.mdx)
- [Read scripts to determine location, set, and design requirements.](Tasks/9305.mdx)
- [Submit plans for approval, and adapt plans to serve intended purposes, or to conform to budget or fabrication restrictions.](Tasks/9293.mdx)
- [Attend rehearsals and production meetings to obtain and share information related to sets.](Tasks/9306.mdx)
- [Confer with clients and staff to gather information about exhibit space, proposed themes and content, timelines, budgets, materials, or promotion requirements.](Tasks/9287.mdx)
- [Research architectural and stylistic elements appropriate to the time period to be depicted, consulting experts for information, as necessary.](Tasks/9297.mdx)
- [Observe sets during rehearsals in order to ensure that set elements do not interfere with performance aspects such as cast movement and camera angles.](Tasks/9303.mdx)
- [Collaborate with those in charge of lighting and sound so that those production aspects can be coordinated with set designs or exhibit layouts.](Tasks/9296.mdx)

# Ophthalmic Medical Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-2057.00

Assist ophthalmologists by performing ophthalmic clinical functions. May administer eye exams, administer eye medications, and instruct the patient in care and use of corrective lenses.

## Tasks

- [Take and document patients' medical histories.](Tasks/19252.mdx)
- [Conduct tonometry or tonography tests to measure intraocular pressure.](Tasks/19257.mdx)
- [Operate ophthalmic equipment, such as autorefractors, phoropters, tomographs, or retinoscopes.](Tasks/19250.mdx)
- [Take anatomical or functional ocular measurements of the eye or surrounding tissue, such as axial length measurements.](Tasks/19251.mdx)
- [Measure visual acuity, including near, distance, pinhole, or dynamic visual acuity, using appropriate tests.](Tasks/19249.mdx)
- [Measure and record lens power, using lensometers.](Tasks/19248.mdx)
- [Administer topical ophthalmic or oral medications.](Tasks/19239.mdx)
- [Conduct visual field tests to measure field of vision.](Tasks/19245.mdx)
- [Assist physicians in performing ophthalmic procedures, including surgery.](Tasks/19255.mdx)
- [Measure corneal curvature with keratometers or ophthalmometers to aid in the diagnosis of conditions, such as astigmatism.](Tasks/19258.mdx)

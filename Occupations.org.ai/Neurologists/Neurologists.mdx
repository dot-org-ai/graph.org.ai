# Neurologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1217.00

Diagnose, manage, and treat disorders and diseases of the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves, with a primarily nonsurgical focus.

## Tasks

- [Interview patients to obtain information, such as complaints, symptoms, medical histories, and family histories.](Tasks/17123.mdx)
- [Examine patients to obtain information about functional status of areas, such as vision, physical strength, coordination, reflexes, sensations, language skills, cognitive abilities, and mental status.](Tasks/17132.mdx)
- [Perform or interpret the outcomes of procedures or diagnostic tests, such as lumbar punctures, electroencephalography, electromyography, and nerve conduction velocity tests.](Tasks/17130.mdx)
- [Order or interpret results of laboratory analyses of patients' blood or cerebrospinal fluid.](Tasks/17121.mdx)
- [Diagnose neurological conditions based on interpretation of examination findings, histories, or test results.](Tasks/17125.mdx)
- [Prescribe or administer medications, such as anti-epileptic drugs, and monitor patients for behavioral and cognitive side effects.](Tasks/17117.mdx)
- [Identify and treat major neurological system diseases and disorders, such as central nervous system infection, cranio spinal trauma, dementia, and stroke.](Tasks/17131.mdx)
- [Develop treatment plans based on diagnoses and on evaluation of factors, such as age and general health, or procedural risks and costs.](Tasks/17126.mdx)
- [Inform patients or families of neurological diagnoses and prognoses, or benefits, risks and costs of various treatment plans.](Tasks/17124.mdx)
- [Prepare, maintain, or review records that include patients' histories, neurological examination findings, treatment plans, or outcomes.](Tasks/17119.mdx)

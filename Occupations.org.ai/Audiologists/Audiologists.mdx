# Audiologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1181.00

Assess and treat persons with hearing and related disorders. May fit hearing aids and provide auditory training. May perform research related to hearing problems.

## Tasks

- [Maintain patient records at all stages, including initial and subsequent evaluation and treatment activities.](Tasks/18690.mdx)
- [Evaluate hearing and balance disorders to determine diagnoses and courses of treatment.](Tasks/18673.mdx)
- [Fit, dispense, and repair assistive devices, such as hearing aids.](Tasks/18681.mdx)
- [Administer hearing tests and examine patients to collect information on type and degree of impairment, using specialized instruments and electronic equipment.](Tasks/18678.mdx)
- [Monitor patients' progress and provide ongoing observation of hearing or balance status.](Tasks/18686.mdx)
- [Instruct patients, parents, teachers, or employers in communication strategies to maximize effective receptive communication.](Tasks/18684.mdx)
- [Counsel and instruct patients and their families in techniques to improve hearing and communication related to hearing loss.](Tasks/18672.mdx)
- [Refer patients to additional medical or educational services, if needed.](Tasks/18688.mdx)
- [Participate in conferences or training to update or share knowledge of new hearing or balance disorder treatment methods or technologies.](Tasks/18675.mdx)
- [Examine and clean patients' ear canals.](Tasks/7870.mdx)

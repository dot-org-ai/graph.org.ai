# Agricultural Engineers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 17-2021.00

Apply knowledge of engineering technology and biological science to agricultural problems concerned with power and machinery, electrification, structures, soil and water conservation, and processing of agricultural products.

## Tasks

- [Prepare reports, sketches, working drawings, specifications, proposals, and budgets for proposed sites or systems.](Tasks/5336.mdx)
- [Visit sites to observe environmental problems, to consult with contractors, or to monitor construction activities.](Tasks/5325.mdx)
- [Meet with clients, such as district or regional councils, farmers, and developers, to discuss their needs.](Tasks/5337.mdx)
- [Discuss plans with clients, contractors, consultants, and other engineers so that they can be evaluated and necessary changes made.](Tasks/5331.mdx)
- [Test agricultural machinery and equipment to ensure adequate performance.](Tasks/5327.mdx)
- [Plan and direct construction of rural electric-power distribution systems, and irrigation, drainage, and flood control systems for soil and water conservation.](Tasks/5334.mdx)
- [Provide advice on water quality and issues related to pollution management, river control, and ground and surface water resources.](Tasks/5329.mdx)
- [Design structures for crop storage, animal shelter and loading, and animal and crop processing, and supervise their construction.](Tasks/5328.mdx)
- [Conduct educational programs that provide farmers or farm cooperative members with information that can help them improve agricultural productivity.](Tasks/5330.mdx)
- [Design sensing, measuring, and recording devices, and other instrumentation used to study plant or animal life.](Tasks/5338.mdx)

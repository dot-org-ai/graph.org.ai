# Nurse Anesthetists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1151.00

Administer anesthesia, monitor patient's vital signs, and oversee patient recovery from anesthesia. May assist anesthesiologists, surgeons, other physicians, or dentists. Must be registered nurses who have specialized graduate education.

## Tasks

- [Manage patients' airway or pulmonary status, using techniques such as endotracheal intubation, mechanical ventilation, pharmacological support, respiratory therapy, and extubation.](Tasks/18361.mdx)
- [Respond to emergency situations by providing airway management, administering emergency fluids or drugs, or using basic or advanced cardiac life support techniques.](Tasks/18365.mdx)
- [Monitor patients' responses, including skin color, pupil dilation, pulse, heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, ventilation, or urine output, using invasive and noninvasive techniques.](Tasks/18362.mdx)
- [Select, order, or administer anesthetics, adjuvant drugs, accessory drugs, fluids or blood products as necessary.](Tasks/18366.mdx)
- [Select, prepare, or use equipment, monitors, supplies, or drugs for the administration of anesthetics.](Tasks/18370.mdx)
- [Assess patients' medical histories to predict anesthesia response.](Tasks/18367.mdx)
- [Perform or manage regional anesthetic techniques, such as local, spinal, epidural, caudal, nerve blocks and intravenous blocks.](Tasks/18363.mdx)
- [Develop anesthesia care plans.](Tasks/18368.mdx)
- [Obtain informed consent from patients for anesthesia procedures.](Tasks/18369.mdx)
- [Prepare prescribed solutions and administer local, intravenous, spinal, or other anesthetics, following specified methods and procedures.](Tasks/18364.mdx)

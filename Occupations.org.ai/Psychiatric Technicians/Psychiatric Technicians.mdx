# Psychiatric Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-2053.00

Care for individuals with mental or emotional conditions or disabilities, following the instructions of physicians or other health practitioners. Monitor patients' physical and emotional well-being and report to medical staff. May participate in rehabilitation and treatment programs, help with personal hygiene, and administer oral or injectable medications.

## Tasks

- [Provide nursing, psychiatric, or personal care to patients with cognitive, intellectual, or developmental disabilities.](Tasks/6945.mdx)
- [Encourage patients to develop work skills and to participate in social, recreational, or other therapeutic activities that enhance interpersonal skills or develop social relationships.](Tasks/6948.mdx)
- [Restrain violent, potentially violent, or suicidal patients by verbal or physical means as required.](Tasks/6951.mdx)
- [Lead prescribed individual or group therapy sessions as part of specific therapeutic procedures.](Tasks/6956.mdx)
- [Monitor patients' physical and emotional well-being and report unusual behavior or physical ailments to medical staff.](Tasks/6944.mdx)
- [Take and record measures of patients' physical condition, using devices such as thermometers or blood pressure gauges.](Tasks/6947.mdx)
- [Observe and influence patients' behavior, communicating and interacting with them and teaching, counseling, or befriending them.](Tasks/6946.mdx)
- [Aid patients in performing tasks, such as bathing or keeping beds, clothing, or living areas clean.](Tasks/6952.mdx)
- [Collaborate with or assist doctors, psychologists, or rehabilitation therapists in working with patients with cognitive, intellectual, or developmental disabilities to treat, rehabilitate, and return patients to the community.](Tasks/6949.mdx)
- [Develop or teach strategies to promote client wellness and independence.](Tasks/6950.mdx)

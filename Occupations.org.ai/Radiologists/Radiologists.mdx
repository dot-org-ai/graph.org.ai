# Radiologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1224.00

Diagnose and treat diseases and injuries using medical imaging techniques, such as x rays, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), nuclear medicine, and ultrasounds. May perform minimally invasive medical procedures and tests.

## Tasks

- [Prepare comprehensive interpretive reports of findings.](Tasks/17242.mdx)
- [Perform or interpret the outcomes of diagnostic imaging procedures including magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), computer tomography (CT), positron emission tomography (PET), nuclear cardiology treadmill studies, mammography, or ultrasound.](Tasks/17254.mdx)
- [Document the performance, interpretation, or outcomes of all procedures performed.](Tasks/17248.mdx)
- [Communicate examination results or diagnostic information to referring physicians, patients, or families.](Tasks/17252.mdx)
- [Obtain patients' histories from electronic records, patient interviews, dictated reports, or by communicating with referring clinicians.](Tasks/17243.mdx)
- [Review or transmit images and information using picture archiving or communications systems.](Tasks/17239.mdx)
- [Confer with medical professionals regarding image-based diagnoses.](Tasks/17245.mdx)
- [Recognize or treat complications during and after procedures, including blood pressure problems, pain, oversedation, or bleeding.](Tasks/17241.mdx)
- [Develop or monitor procedures to ensure adequate quality control of images.](Tasks/17249.mdx)
- [Provide counseling to radiologic patients to explain the processes, risks, benefits, or alternative treatments.](Tasks/17251.mdx)

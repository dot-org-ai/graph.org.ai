# Nurse Practitioners

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1171.00

Diagnose and treat acute, episodic, or chronic illness, independently or as part of a healthcare team. May focus on health promotion and disease prevention. May order, perform, or interpret diagnostic tests such as lab work and x rays. May prescribe medication. Must be registered nurses who have specialized graduate education.

## Tasks

- [Maintain complete and detailed records of patients' health care plans and prognoses.](Tasks/18399.mdx)
- [Develop treatment plans, based on scientific rationale, standards of care, and professional practice guidelines.](Tasks/18389.mdx)
- [Provide patients with information needed to promote health, reduce risk factors, or prevent disease or disability.](Tasks/18393.mdx)
- [Analyze and interpret patients' histories, symptoms, physical findings, or diagnostic information to develop appropriate diagnoses.](Tasks/18382.mdx)
- [Diagnose or treat complex, unstable, comorbid, episodic, or emergency conditions in collaboration with other health care providers as necessary.](Tasks/18385.mdx)
- [Prescribe medication dosages, routes, and frequencies, based on such patient characteristics as age and gender.](Tasks/18396.mdx)
- [Diagnose or treat chronic health care problems, such as high blood pressure and diabetes.](Tasks/18384.mdx)
- [Prescribe medications based on efficacy, safety, and cost as legally authorized.](Tasks/18392.mdx)
- [Recommend diagnostic or therapeutic interventions with attention to safety, cost, invasiveness, simplicity, acceptability, adherence, and efficacy.](Tasks/18394.mdx)
- [Detect and respond to adverse drug reactions, with special attention to vulnerable populations such as infants, children, pregnant and lactating women, or older adults.](Tasks/18388.mdx)

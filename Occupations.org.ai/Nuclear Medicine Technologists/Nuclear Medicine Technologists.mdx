# Nuclear Medicine Technologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-2033.00

Prepare, administer, and measure radioactive isotopes in therapeutic, diagnostic, and tracer studies using a variety of radioisotope equipment. Prepare stock solutions of radioactive materials and calculate doses to be administered by radiologists. Subject patients to radiation. Execute blood volume, red cell survival, and fat absorption studies following standard laboratory techniques.

## Tasks

- [Administer radiopharmaceuticals or radiation intravenously to detect or treat diseases, using radioisotope equipment, under direction of a physician.](Tasks/15239.mdx)
- [Detect and map radiopharmaceuticals in patients' bodies, using a camera to produce photographic or computer images.](Tasks/4149.mdx)
- [Process cardiac function studies, using computer.](Tasks/4153.mdx)
- [Calculate, measure, and record radiation dosage or radiopharmaceuticals received, used, and disposed, using computer and following physician's prescription.](Tasks/4148.mdx)
- [Record and process results of procedures.](Tasks/4155.mdx)
- [Produce a computer-generated or film image for interpretation by a physician.](Tasks/4152.mdx)
- [Prepare stock radiopharmaceuticals, adhering to safety standards that minimize radiation exposure to workers and patients.](Tasks/4156.mdx)
- [Explain test procedures and safety precautions to patients and provide them with assistance during test procedures.](Tasks/4150.mdx)
- [Perform quality control checks on laboratory equipment or cameras.](Tasks/15240.mdx)
- [Dispose of radioactive materials and store radiopharmaceuticals, following radiation safety procedures.](Tasks/4154.mdx)

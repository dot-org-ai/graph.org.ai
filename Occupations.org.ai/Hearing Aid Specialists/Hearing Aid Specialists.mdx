# Hearing Aid Specialists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-2092.00

Select and fit hearing aids for customers. Administer and interpret tests of hearing. Assess hearing instrument efficacy. Take ear impressions and prepare, design, and modify ear molds.

## Tasks

- [Train clients to use hearing aids or other augmentative communication devices.](Tasks/17441.mdx)
- [Counsel patients and families on communication strategies and the effects of hearing loss.](Tasks/21189.mdx)
- [Select and administer tests to evaluate hearing or related disabilities.](Tasks/17439.mdx)
- [Administer basic hearing tests including air conduction, bone conduction, or speech audiometry tests.](Tasks/17440.mdx)
- [Maintain or repair hearing aids or other communication devices.](Tasks/17443.mdx)
- [Perform basic screening procedures, such as pure tone screening, otoacoustic screening, immittance screening, and screening of ear canal status using otoscope.](Tasks/17446.mdx)
- [Create or modify impressions for earmolds and hearing aid shells.](Tasks/17442.mdx)
- [Read current literature, talk with colleagues, and participate in professional organizations or conferences to keep abreast of developments in audiology.](Tasks/17448.mdx)
- [Demonstrate assistive listening devices (ALDs) to clients.](Tasks/17444.mdx)
- [Assist audiologists in performing aural procedures, such as real ear measurements, speech audiometry, auditory brainstem responses, electronystagmography, and cochlear implant mapping.](Tasks/17447.mdx)

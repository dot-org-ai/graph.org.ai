# Pediatric Surgeons

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1243.00

Diagnose and perform surgery to treat fetal abnormalities and birth defects, diseases, and injuries in fetuses, premature and newborn infants, children, and adolescents. Includes all pediatric surgical specialties and subspecialties.

## Tasks

- [Analyze patient's medical history, medication allergies, physical condition, and examination results to verify operation's necessity and to determine best procedure.](Tasks/22760.mdx)
- [Conduct research to develop and test surgical techniques that can improve operating procedures and outcomes.](Tasks/22761.mdx)
- [Consult with patient's other medical care specialists, such as cardiologist and endocrinologist, to determine if surgery is necessary.](Tasks/22762.mdx)
- [Describe preoperative and postoperative treatments and procedures, such as sedatives, diets, antibiotics, or preparation and treatment of the patient's operative area, to parents or guardians of the patient.](Tasks/22763.mdx)
- [Direct and coordinate activities of nurses, assistants, specialists, residents, and other medical staff.](Tasks/22764.mdx)
- [Examine fetuses, infants, children, and adolescents, and diagnose health issues to determine need for intervention, such as surgery.](Tasks/22765.mdx)
- [Examine instruments, equipment, and operating room to ensure sterility.](Tasks/22766.mdx)
- [Examine patient to obtain information on medical condition and surgical risk.](Tasks/22767.mdx)
- [Follow established surgical techniques during the operation.](Tasks/22768.mdx)
- [Inform parents and guardians of child's health problems and surgical procedures through various channels, such as in-person and telecommunication systems.](Tasks/22769.mdx)

# Regulatory Affairs Specialists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 13-1041.07

Coordinate and document internal regulatory processes, such as internal audits, inspections, license renewals, or registrations. May compile and prepare materials for submission to regulatory agencies.

## Tasks

- [Coordinate efforts associated with the preparation of regulatory documents or submissions.](Tasks/18054.mdx)
- [Communicate with regulatory agencies regarding pre-submission strategies, potential regulatory pathways, compliance test requirements, or clarification and follow-up of submissions under review.](Tasks/18045.mdx)
- [Prepare or direct the preparation of additional information or responses as requested by regulatory agencies.](Tasks/18061.mdx)
- [Coordinate, prepare, or review regulatory submissions for domestic or international projects.](Tasks/18048.mdx)
- [Prepare or maintain technical files as necessary to obtain and sustain product approval.](Tasks/18062.mdx)
- [Interpret regulatory rules or rule changes and ensure that they are communicated through corporate policies and procedures.](Tasks/18049.mdx)
- [Determine the types of regulatory submissions or internal documentation that are required in situations such as proposed device changes or labeling changes.](Tasks/18055.mdx)
- [Coordinate recall or market withdrawal activities as necessary.](Tasks/18067.mdx)
- [Advise project teams on subjects such as premarket regulatory requirements, export and labeling requirements, or clinical study compliance issues.](Tasks/18052.mdx)
- [Review adverse drug reactions and file all related reports in accordance with regulatory agency guidelines.](Tasks/18070.mdx)

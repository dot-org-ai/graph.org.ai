# Administrative Law Judges, Adjudicators, and Hearing Officers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 23-1021.00

Conduct hearings to recommend or make decisions on claims concerning government programs or other government-related matters. Determine liability, sanctions, or penalties, or recommend the acceptance or rejection of claims or settlements.

## Tasks

- [Determine existence and amount of liability according to current laws, administrative and judicial precedents, and available evidence.](Tasks/7627.mdx)
- [Monitor and direct the activities of trials and hearings to ensure that they are conducted fairly and that courts administer justice while safeguarding the legal rights of all involved parties.](Tasks/7623.mdx)
- [Prepare written opinions and decisions.](Tasks/7617.mdx)
- [Authorize payment of valid claims and determine method of payment.](Tasks/7624.mdx)
- [Conduct hearings to review and decide claims regarding issues, such as social program eligibility, environmental protection, or enforcement of health and safety regulations.](Tasks/7625.mdx)
- [Research and analyze laws, regulations, policies, and precedent decisions to prepare for hearings and to determine conclusions.](Tasks/7619.mdx)
- [Review and evaluate data on documents, such as claim applications, birth or death certificates, or physician or employer records.](Tasks/7618.mdx)
- [Recommend the acceptance or rejection of claims or compromise settlements according to laws, regulations, policies, and precedent decisions.](Tasks/7621.mdx)
- [Rule on exceptions, motions, and admissibility of evidence.](Tasks/7626.mdx)
- [Explain to claimants how they can appeal rulings that go against them.](Tasks/7622.mdx)

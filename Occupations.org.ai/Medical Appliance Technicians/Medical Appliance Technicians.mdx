# Medical Appliance Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 51-9082.00

Construct, maintain, or repair medical supportive devices such as braces, orthotics and prosthetic devices, joints, arch supports, and other surgical and medical appliances.

## Tasks

- [Drill and tap holes for rivets, and glue, weld, bolt, or rivet parts together to form prosthetic or orthotic devices.](Tasks/10520.mdx)
- [Read prescriptions or specifications to determine the type of product or device to be fabricated and the materials and tools required.](Tasks/10514.mdx)
- [Make orthotic or prosthetic devices, using materials such as thermoplastic and thermosetting materials, metal alloys and leather, and hand or power tools.](Tasks/10513.mdx)
- [Bend, form, and shape fabric or material to conform to prescribed contours of structural components.](Tasks/10519.mdx)
- [Construct or receive casts or impressions of patients' torsos or limbs for use as cutting and fabrication patterns.](Tasks/10518.mdx)
- [Repair, modify, or maintain medical supportive devices, such as artificial limbs, braces, or surgical supports, according to specifications.](Tasks/10515.mdx)
- [Cover or pad metal or plastic structures or devices, using coverings such as rubber, leather, felt, plastic, or fiberglass.](Tasks/10523.mdx)
- [Test medical supportive devices for proper alignment, movement, or biomechanical stability, using meters and alignment fixtures.](Tasks/10522.mdx)
- [Lay out and mark dimensions of parts, using templates and precision measuring instruments.](Tasks/10521.mdx)
- [Fit appliances onto patients, and make any necessary adjustments.](Tasks/10512.mdx)

# Captains, Mates, and Pilots of Water Vessels

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 53-5021.00

Command or supervise operations of ships and water vessels, such as tugboats and ferryboats. Required to hold license issued by U.S. Coast Guard.

## Tasks

- [Direct courses and speeds of ships, based on specialized knowledge of local winds, weather, water depths, tides, currents, and hazards.](Tasks/23802.mdx)
- [Prevent ships under navigational control from engaging in unsafe operations.](Tasks/23803.mdx)
- [Serve as a vessel's docking master upon arrival at a port or at a berth.](Tasks/23804.mdx)
- [Consult maps, charts, weather reports, or navigation equipment to determine and direct ship movements.](Tasks/23805.mdx)
- [Steer and operate vessels, using radios, depth finders, radars, lights, buoys, or lighthouses.](Tasks/23806.mdx)
- [Operate ship-to-shore radios to exchange information needed for ship operations.](Tasks/23807.mdx)
- [Dock or undock vessels, sometimes maneuvering through narrow spaces, such as locks.](Tasks/23808.mdx)
- [Stand watches on vessels during specified periods while vessels are under way.](Tasks/23809.mdx)
- [Inspect vessels to ensure efficient and safe operation of vessels and equipment and conformance to regulations.](Tasks/23810.mdx)
- [Read gauges to verify sufficient levels of hydraulic fluid, air pressure, or oxygen.](Tasks/23811.mdx)

# Atmospheric and Space Scientists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 19-2021.00

Investigate atmospheric phenomena and interpret meteorological data, gathered by surface and air stations, satellites, and radar to prepare reports and forecasts for public and other uses. Includes weather analysts and forecasters whose functions require the detailed knowledge of meteorology.

## Tasks

- [Develop or use mathematical or computer models for weather forecasting.](Tasks/20208.mdx)
- [Interpret data, reports, maps, photographs, or charts to predict long- or short-range weather conditions, using computer models and knowledge of climate theory, physics, and mathematics.](Tasks/20209.mdx)
- [Conduct meteorological research into the processes or determinants of atmospheric phenomena, weather, or climate.](Tasks/20210.mdx)
- [Formulate predictions by interpreting environmental data, such as meteorological, atmospheric, oceanic, paleoclimate, climate, or related information.](Tasks/19759.mdx)
- [Broadcast weather conditions, forecasts, or severe weather warnings to the public via television, radio, or the Internet or provide this information to the news media.](Tasks/9068.mdx)
- [Prepare forecasts or briefings to meet the needs of industry, business, government, or other groups.](Tasks/9070.mdx)
- [Gather data from sources such as surface or upper air stations, satellites, weather bureaus, or radar for use in meteorological reports or forecasts.](Tasks/9069.mdx)
- [Develop computer programs to collect meteorological data or to present meteorological information.](Tasks/21104.mdx)
- [Prepare weather reports or maps for analysis, distribution, or use in weather broadcasts, using computer graphics.](Tasks/20206.mdx)
- [Develop and deliver training on weather topics.](Tasks/21105.mdx)

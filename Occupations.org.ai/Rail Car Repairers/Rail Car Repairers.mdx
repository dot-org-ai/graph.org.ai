# Rail Car Repairers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 49-3043.00

Diagnose, adjust, repair, or overhaul railroad rolling stock, mine cars, or mass transit rail cars.

## Tasks

- [Record conditions of cars, and repair and maintenance work performed or to be performed.](Tasks/9986.mdx)
- [Inspect components such as bearings, seals, gaskets, wheels, and coupler assemblies to determine if repairs are needed.](Tasks/9988.mdx)
- [Repair or replace defective or worn parts such as bearings, pistons, and gears, using hand tools, torque wrenches, power tools, and welding equipment.](Tasks/9984.mdx)
- [Inspect the interior and exterior of rail cars coming into rail yards to identify defects and to determine the extent of wear and damage.](Tasks/9989.mdx)
- [Remove locomotives, car mechanical units, or other components, using pneumatic hoists and jacks, pinch bars, hand tools, and cutting torches.](Tasks/9987.mdx)
- [Test units for operability before and after repairs.](Tasks/9985.mdx)
- [Adjust repaired or replaced units as needed to ensure proper operation.](Tasks/9990.mdx)
- [Repair, fabricate, and install steel or wood fittings, using blueprints, shop sketches, and instruction manuals.](Tasks/9992.mdx)
- [Perform scheduled maintenance, and clean units and components.](Tasks/9991.mdx)
- [Examine car roofs for wear and damage, and repair defective sections, using roofing material, cement, nails, and waterproof paint.](Tasks/10000.mdx)

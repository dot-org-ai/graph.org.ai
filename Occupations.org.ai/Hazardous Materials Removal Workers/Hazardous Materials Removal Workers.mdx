# Hazardous Materials Removal Workers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 47-4041.00

Identify, remove, pack, transport, or dispose of hazardous materials, including asbestos, lead-based paint, waste oil, fuel, transmission fluid, radioactive materials, or contaminated soil. Specialized training and certification in hazardous materials handling or a confined entry permit are generally required. May operate earth-moving equipment or trucks.

## Tasks

- [Build containment areas prior to beginning abatement or decontamination work.](Tasks/20796.mdx)
- [Remove asbestos or lead from surfaces, using hand or power tools such as scrapers, vacuums, or high-pressure sprayers.](Tasks/11591.mdx)
- [Identify asbestos, lead, or other hazardous materials to be removed, using monitoring devices.](Tasks/11594.mdx)
- [Prepare hazardous material for removal or storage.](Tasks/20797.mdx)
- [Comply with prescribed safety procedures or federal laws regulating waste disposal methods.](Tasks/11584.mdx)
- [Load or unload materials into containers or onto trucks, using hoists or forklifts.](Tasks/11588.mdx)
- [Clean contaminated equipment or areas for reuse, using detergents or solvents, sandblasters, filter pumps, or steam cleaners.](Tasks/11589.mdx)
- [Remove or limit contamination following emergencies involving hazardous substances.](Tasks/19872.mdx)
- [Clean mold-contaminated sites by removing damaged porous materials or thoroughly cleaning all contaminated nonporous materials.](Tasks/19869.mdx)
- [Operate machines or equipment to remove, package, store, or transport loads of waste materials.](Tasks/11587.mdx)

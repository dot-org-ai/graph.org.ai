# Forest and Conservation Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 19-4071.00

Provide technical assistance regarding the conservation of soil, water, forests, or related natural resources. May compile data pertaining to size, content, condition, and other characteristics of forest tracts under the direction of foresters, or train and lead forest workers in forest propagation and fire prevention and suppression. May assist conservation scientists in managing, improving, and protecting rangelands and wildlife habitats.

## Tasks

- [Thin and space trees and control weeds and undergrowth, using manual tools and chemicals, or supervise workers performing these tasks.](Tasks/5566.mdx)
- [Train and lead forest and conservation workers in seasonal activities, such as planting tree seedlings, putting out forest fires, and maintaining recreational facilities.](Tasks/5563.mdx)
- [Provide information about, and enforce, regulations, such as those concerning environmental protection, resource utilization, fire safety, and accident prevention.](Tasks/5570.mdx)
- [Patrol park or forest areas to protect resources and prevent damage.](Tasks/5569.mdx)
- [Map forest tract data using digital mapping systems.](Tasks/21061.mdx)
- [Keep records of the amount and condition of logs taken to mills.](Tasks/5571.mdx)
- [Manage forest protection activities, including fire control, fire crew training, and coordination of fire detection and public education programs.](Tasks/5567.mdx)
- [Monitor activities of logging companies and contractors.](Tasks/5564.mdx)
- [Perform reforestation or forest renewal, including nursery and silviculture operations, site preparation, seeding and tree planting programs, cone collection, and tree improvement.](Tasks/5578.mdx)
- [Plan and supervise construction of access routes and forest roads.](Tasks/5576.mdx)

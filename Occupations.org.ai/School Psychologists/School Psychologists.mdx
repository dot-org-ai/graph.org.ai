# School Psychologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 19-3034.00

Diagnose and implement individual or schoolwide interventions or strategies to address educational, behavioral, or developmental issues that adversely impact educational functioning in a school. May address student learning and behavioral problems and counsel students or families. May design and implement performance plans, and evaluate performance. May consult with other school-based personnel.

## Tasks

- [Compile and interpret students' test results, along with information from teachers and parents, to diagnose conditions and to help assess eligibility for special services.](Tasks/5446.mdx)
- [Maintain student records, including special education reports, confidential records, records of services provided, and behavioral data.](Tasks/5455.mdx)
- [Report any pertinent information to the proper authorities in cases of child endangerment, neglect, or abuse.](Tasks/5447.mdx)
- [Select, administer, and score psychological tests.](Tasks/5449.mdx)
- [Interpret test results and prepare psychological reports for teachers, administrators, and parents.](Tasks/18619.mdx)
- [Assess an individual child's needs, limitations, and potential, using observation, review of school records, and consultation with parents and school personnel.](Tasks/5448.mdx)
- [Develop individualized educational plans in collaboration with teachers and other staff members.](Tasks/5454.mdx)
- [Counsel children and families to help solve conflicts and problems in learning and adjustment.](Tasks/5453.mdx)
- [Collect and analyze data to evaluate the effectiveness of academic programs and other services, such as behavioral management systems.](Tasks/5461.mdx)
- [Provide consultation to parents, teachers, administrators, and others on topics such as learning styles and behavior modification techniques.](Tasks/5450.mdx)

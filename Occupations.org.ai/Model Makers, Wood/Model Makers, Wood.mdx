# Model Makers, Wood

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 51-7031.00

Construct full-size and scale wooden precision models of products. Includes wood jig builders and loft workers.

## Tasks

- [Read blueprints, drawings, or written specifications, and consult with designers to determine sizes and shapes of patterns and required machine setups.](Tasks/14273.mdx)
- [Fit, fasten, and assemble wood parts together to form patterns, models, or sections, using glue, nails, dowels, bolts, screws, and other fasteners.](Tasks/14275.mdx)
- [Verify dimensions and contours of models during hand-forming processes, using templates and measuring devices.](Tasks/14272.mdx)
- [Trim, smooth, and shape surfaces, and plane, shave, file, scrape, and sand models to attain specified shapes, using hand tools.](Tasks/14276.mdx)
- [Plan, lay out, and draw outlines of units, sectional patterns, or full-scale mock-ups of products.](Tasks/14280.mdx)
- [Construct wooden models, patterns, templates, full scale mock-ups, and molds for parts of products and production tools.](Tasks/14278.mdx)
- [Select wooden stock, determine layouts, and mark layouts of parts on stock, using precision equipment such as scribers, squares, and protractors.](Tasks/14277.mdx)
- [Mark identifying information on patterns, parts, and templates to indicate assembly methods and details.](Tasks/14279.mdx)
- [Set up, operate, and adjust a variety of woodworking machines such as bandsaws and planers to cut and shape sections, parts, and patterns, according to specifications.](Tasks/14274.mdx)
- [Maintain pattern records for reference.](Tasks/14282.mdx)

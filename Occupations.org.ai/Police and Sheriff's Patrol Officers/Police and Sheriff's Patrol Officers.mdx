# Police and Sheriff's Patrol Officers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 33-3051.00

Maintain order and protect life and property by enforcing local, tribal, state, or federal laws and ordinances. Perform a combination of the following duties: patrol a specific area; direct traffic; issue traffic summonses; investigate accidents; apprehend and arrest suspects, or serve legal processes of courts. Includes police officers working at educational institutions.

## Tasks

- [Identify, pursue, and arrest suspects and perpetrators of criminal acts.](Tasks/23051.mdx)
- [Provide for public safety by maintaining order, responding to emergencies, protecting people and property, enforcing motor vehicle and criminal laws, and promoting good community relations.](Tasks/23052.mdx)
- [Record facts to prepare reports that document incidents and activities.](Tasks/23053.mdx)
- [Render aid to accident survivors and other persons requiring first aid for physical injuries.](Tasks/23054.mdx)
- [Review facts of incidents to determine if criminal act or statute violations were involved.](Tasks/23056.mdx)
- [Investigate illegal or suspicious activities.](Tasks/23055.mdx)
- [Monitor, note, report, and investigate suspicious persons and situations, safety hazards, and unusual or illegal activity in patrol area.](Tasks/23057.mdx)
- [Testify in court to present evidence or act as witness in traffic and criminal cases.](Tasks/23058.mdx)
- [Relay complaint and emergency-request information to appropriate agency dispatchers.](Tasks/23061.mdx)
- [Monitor traffic to ensure motorists observe traffic regulations and exhibit safe driving procedures.](Tasks/23060.mdx)

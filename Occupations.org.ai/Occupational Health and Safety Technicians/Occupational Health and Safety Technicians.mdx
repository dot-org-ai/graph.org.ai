# Occupational Health and Safety Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 19-5012.00

Collect data on work environments for analysis by occupational health and safety specialists. Implement and conduct evaluation of programs designed to limit chemical, physical, biological, and ergonomic risks to workers.

## Tasks

- [Evaluate situations or make determinations when a worker has refused to work on the grounds that danger or potential harm exists.](Tasks/20249.mdx)
- [Supply, operate, or maintain personal protective equipment.](Tasks/11083.mdx)
- [Train workers in safety procedures related to green jobs, such as the use of fall protection devices or maintenance of proper ventilation during wind turbine construction.](Tasks/19818.mdx)
- [Test workplaces for environmental hazards, such as exposure to radiation, chemical or biological hazards, or excessive noise.](Tasks/11087.mdx)
- [Maintain all required environmental records and documentation.](Tasks/11082.mdx)
- [Provide consultation to organizations or agencies on the workplace application of safety principles, practices, or techniques.](Tasks/20253.mdx)
- [Inspect fire suppression systems or portable fire systems to ensure proper working order.](Tasks/20252.mdx)
- [Verify availability or monitor use of safety equipment, such as hearing protection or respirators.](Tasks/20248.mdx)
- [Recommend corrective measures to be applied based on results of environmental contaminant analyses.](Tasks/20250.mdx)
- [Prepare or review specifications or orders for the purchase of safety equipment, ensuring that proper features are present and that items conform to health and safety standards.](Tasks/11088.mdx)

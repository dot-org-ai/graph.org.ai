# Furniture Finishers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 51-7021.00

Shape, finish, and refinish damaged, worn, or used furniture or new high-grade furniture to specified color or finish.

## Tasks

- [Brush, spray, or hand-rub finishing ingredients, such as paint, oil, stain, or wax, onto and into wood grain and apply lacquer or other sealers.](Tasks/14251.mdx)
- [Fill and smooth cracks or depressions, remove marks and imperfections, and repair broken parts, using plastic or wood putty, glue, nails, or screws.](Tasks/14254.mdx)
- [Smooth, shape, and touch up surfaces to prepare them for finishing, using sandpaper, pumice stones, steel wool, chisels, sanders, or grinders.](Tasks/14252.mdx)
- [Remove accessories prior to finishing, and mask areas that should not be exposed to finishing processes or substances.](Tasks/14263.mdx)
- [Remove old finishes and damaged or deteriorated parts, using hand tools, stripping tools, sandpaper, steel wool, abrasives, solvents, or dip baths.](Tasks/14259.mdx)
- [Treat warped or stained surfaces to restore original contours and colors.](Tasks/14264.mdx)
- [Select appropriate finishing ingredients such as paint, stain, lacquer, shellac, or varnish, depending on factors such as wood hardness and surface type.](Tasks/14253.mdx)
- [Mix finish ingredients to obtain desired colors or shades.](Tasks/14250.mdx)
- [Remove excess solvent, using cloths soaked in paint thinner.](Tasks/14267.mdx)
- [Examine furniture to determine the extent of damage or deterioration, and to decide on the best method for repair or restoration.](Tasks/14256.mdx)

# Low Vision Therapists, Orientation and Mobility Specialists, and Vision Rehabilitation Therapists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1122.01

Provide therapy to patients with visual impairments to improve their functioning in daily life activities. May train patients in activities such as computer use, communication skills, or home management skills.

## Tasks

- [Teach cane skills, including cane use with a guide, diagonal techniques, and two-point touches.](Tasks/17336.mdx)
- [Recommend appropriate mobility devices or systems, such as human guides, dog guides, long canes, electronic travel aids (ETAs), and other adaptive mobility devices (AMDs).](Tasks/17355.mdx)
- [Train clients with visual impairments to use mobility devices or systems, such as human guides, dog guides, electronic travel aids (ETAs), and other adaptive mobility devices (AMDs).](Tasks/17352.mdx)
- [Develop rehabilitation or instructional plans collaboratively with clients, based on results of assessments, needs, and goals.](Tasks/17350.mdx)
- [Write reports or complete forms to document assessments, training, progress, or follow-up outcomes.](Tasks/17349.mdx)
- [Train clients to use tactile, auditory, kinesthetic, olfactory, and proprioceptive information.](Tasks/17346.mdx)
- [Assess clients' functioning in areas such as vision, orientation and mobility skills, social and emotional issues, cognition, physical abilities, and personal goals.](Tasks/17351.mdx)
- [Teach clients to travel independently, using a variety of actual or simulated travel situations or exercises.](Tasks/17345.mdx)
- [Teach self-advocacy skills to clients.](Tasks/23918.mdx)
- [Provide consultation, support, or education to groups such as parents and teachers.](Tasks/17338.mdx)

# Bioengineers and Biomedical Engineers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 17-2031.00

Apply knowledge of engineering, biology, chemistry, computer science, and biomechanical principles to the design, development, and evaluation of biological, agricultural, and health systems and products, such as artificial organs, prostheses, instrumentation, medical information systems, and health management and care delivery systems.

## Tasks

- [Evaluate the safety, efficiency, and effectiveness of biomedical equipment.](Tasks/8968.mdx)
- [Prepare technical reports, data summary documents, or research articles for scientific publication, regulatory submissions, or patent applications.](Tasks/21856.mdx)
- [Design or develop medical diagnostic or clinical instrumentation, equipment, or procedures, using the principles of engineering and biobehavioral sciences.](Tasks/21847.mdx)
- [Conduct research, along with life scientists, chemists, and medical scientists, on the engineering aspects of the biological systems of humans and animals.](Tasks/8975.mdx)
- [Adapt or design computer hardware or software for medical science uses.](Tasks/8979.mdx)
- [Maintain databases of experiment characteristics or results.](Tasks/21853.mdx)
- [Develop statistical models or simulations, using statistical or modeling software.](Tasks/21851.mdx)
- [Read current scientific or trade literature to stay abreast of scientific, industrial, or technological advances.](Tasks/21857.mdx)
- [Manage teams of engineers by creating schedules, tracking inventory, creating or using budgets, or overseeing contract obligations or deadlines.](Tasks/21854.mdx)
- [Develop models or computer simulations of human biobehavioral systems to obtain data for measuring or controlling life processes.](Tasks/8973.mdx)

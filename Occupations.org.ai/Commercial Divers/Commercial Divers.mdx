# Commercial Divers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 49-9092.00

Work below surface of water, using surface-supplied air or scuba equipment to inspect, repair, remove, or install equipment and structures. May use a variety of power and hand tools, such as drills, sledgehammers, torches, and welding equipment. May conduct tests or experiments, rig explosives, or photograph structures or marine life.

## Tasks

- [Take appropriate safety precautions, such as monitoring dive lengths and depths and registering with authorities before diving expeditions begin.](Tasks/13855.mdx)
- [Check and maintain diving equipment, such as helmets, masks, air tanks, harnesses, or gauges.](Tasks/13856.mdx)
- [Communicate with workers on the surface while underwater, using signal lines or telephones.](Tasks/13854.mdx)
- [Descend into water with the aid of diver helpers, using scuba gear or diving suits.](Tasks/13857.mdx)
- [Obtain information about diving tasks and environmental conditions.](Tasks/13858.mdx)
- [Supervise or train other divers, including hobby divers.](Tasks/13871.mdx)
- [Inspect the condition of underwater steel or wood structures.](Tasks/20809.mdx)
- [Inspect and test docks, ships, buoyage systems, plant intakes or outflows, or underwater pipelines, cables, or sewers, using closed circuit television, still photography, and testing equipment.](Tasks/13859.mdx)
- [Repair ships, bridge foundations, or other structures below the water line, using caulk, bolts, and hand tools.](Tasks/13860.mdx)
- [Recover objects by placing rigging around sunken objects, hooking rigging to crane lines, and operating winches, derricks, or cranes to raise objects.](Tasks/13862.mdx)

# Radiation Therapists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1124.00

Provide radiation therapy to patients as prescribed by a radiation oncologist according to established practices and standards. Duties may include reviewing prescription and diagnosis; acting as liaison with physician and supportive care personnel; preparing equipment, such as immobilization, treatment, and protection devices; and maintaining records, reports, and files. May assist in dosimetry procedures and tumor localization.

## Tasks

- [Position patients for treatment with accuracy, according to prescription.](Tasks/4086.mdx)
- [Administer prescribed doses of radiation to specific body parts, using radiation therapy equipment according to established practices and standards.](Tasks/4085.mdx)
- [Follow principles of radiation protection for patient, self, and others.](Tasks/4088.mdx)
- [Review prescription, diagnosis, patient chart, and identification.](Tasks/4090.mdx)
- [Conduct most treatment sessions independently, in accordance with the long-term treatment plan and under the general direction of the patient's physician.](Tasks/4091.mdx)
- [Enter data into computer and set controls to operate or adjust equipment or regulate dosage.](Tasks/4087.mdx)
- [Check radiation therapy equipment to ensure proper operation.](Tasks/4092.mdx)
- [Observe and reassure patients during treatment and report unusual reactions to physician or turn equipment off if unexpected adverse reactions occur.](Tasks/4093.mdx)
- [Educate, prepare, and reassure patients and their families by answering questions, providing physical assistance, and reinforcing physicians' advice regarding treatment reactions or post-treatment care.](Tasks/4095.mdx)
- [Maintain records, reports, or files as required, including such information as radiation dosages, equipment settings, or patients' reactions.](Tasks/4089.mdx)

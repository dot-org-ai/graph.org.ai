# Veterinarians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1131.00

Diagnose, treat, or research diseases and injuries of animals. Includes veterinarians who conduct research and development, inspect livestock, or care for pets and companion animals.

## Tasks

- [Treat sick or injured animals by prescribing medication, setting bones, dressing wounds, or performing surgery.](Tasks/4107.mdx)
- [Inoculate animals against various diseases, such as rabies or distemper.](Tasks/4108.mdx)
- [Examine animals to detect and determine the nature of diseases or injuries.](Tasks/4106.mdx)
- [Collect body tissue, feces, blood, urine, or other body fluids for examination and analysis.](Tasks/4109.mdx)
- [Operate diagnostic equipment, such as radiographic or ultrasound equipment, and interpret the resulting images.](Tasks/4110.mdx)
- [Educate the public about diseases that can be spread from animals to humans.](Tasks/4112.mdx)
- [Counsel clients about the deaths of their pets or about euthanasia decisions for their pets.](Tasks/20412.mdx)
- [Advise animal owners regarding sanitary measures, feeding, general care, medical conditions, or treatment options.](Tasks/18532.mdx)
- [Euthanize animals.](Tasks/4115.mdx)
- [Attend lectures, conferences, or continuing education courses.](Tasks/18533.mdx)

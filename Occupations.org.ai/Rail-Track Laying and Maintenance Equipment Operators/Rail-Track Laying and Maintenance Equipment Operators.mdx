# Rail-Track Laying and Maintenance Equipment Operators

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 47-4061.00

Lay, repair, and maintain track for standard or narrow-gauge railroad equipment used in regular railroad service or in plant yards, quarries, sand and gravel pits, and mines. Includes ballast cleaning machine operators and railroad bed tamping machine operators.

## Tasks

- [Patrol assigned track sections so that damaged or broken track can be located and reported.](Tasks/13643.mdx)
- [Repair or adjust track switches, using wrenches and replacement parts.](Tasks/13645.mdx)
- [Weld sections of track together, such as switch points and frogs.](Tasks/20798.mdx)
- [Observe leveling indicator arms to verify levelness and alignment of tracks.](Tasks/13654.mdx)
- [Operate single- or multiple-head spike driving machines to drive spikes into ties and secure rails.](Tasks/13655.mdx)
- [Operate track wrenches to tighten or loosen bolts at joints that hold ends of rails together.](Tasks/13653.mdx)
- [Cut rails to specified lengths, using rail saws.](Tasks/13648.mdx)
- [Lubricate machines, change oil, or fill hydraulic reservoirs to specified levels.](Tasks/13646.mdx)
- [Drill holes through rails, tie plates, or fishplates for insertion of bolts or spikes, using power drills.](Tasks/13651.mdx)
- [Clean tracks or clear ice or snow from tracks or switch boxes.](Tasks/13644.mdx)

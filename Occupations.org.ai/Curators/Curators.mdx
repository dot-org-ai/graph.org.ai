# Curators

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 25-4012.00

Administer collections, such as artwork, collectibles, historic items, or scientific specimens of museums or other institutions. May conduct instructional, research, or public service activities of institution.

## Tasks

- [Plan and organize the acquisition, storage, and exhibition of collections and related materials, including the selection of exhibition themes and designs, and develop or install exhibit materials.](Tasks/18641.mdx)
- [Develop and maintain an institution's registration, cataloging, and basic record-keeping systems, using computer databases.](Tasks/1644.mdx)
- [Plan and conduct special research projects in area of interest or expertise.](Tasks/1649.mdx)
- [Provide information from the institution's holdings to other curators and to the public.](Tasks/1645.mdx)
- [Negotiate and authorize purchase, sale, exchange, or loan of collections.](Tasks/1648.mdx)
- [Study, examine, and test acquisitions to authenticate their origin, composition, history, and to assess their current value.](Tasks/1655.mdx)
- [Inspect premises to assess the need for repairs and to ensure that climate and pest control issues are addressed.](Tasks/1646.mdx)
- [Write and review grant proposals, journal articles, institutional reports, and publicity materials.](Tasks/1654.mdx)
- [Design, organize, or conduct tours, workshops, and instructional or educational sessions to acquaint individuals with an institution's facilities and materials.](Tasks/18642.mdx)
- [Attend meetings, conventions, and civic events to promote use of institution's services, to seek financing, and to maintain community alliances.](Tasks/1652.mdx)

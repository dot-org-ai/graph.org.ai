# Recreational Therapists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1125.00

Plan, direct, or coordinate medically-approved recreation programs for patients in hospitals, nursing homes, or other institutions. Activities include sports, trips, dramatics, social activities, and crafts. May assess a patient condition and recommend appropriate recreational activity.

## Tasks

- [Instruct patient in activities and techniques, such as sports, dance, music, art, or relaxation techniques, designed to meet their specific physical or psychological needs.](Tasks/393.mdx)
- [Conduct therapy sessions to improve patients' mental and physical well-being.](Tasks/392.mdx)
- [Plan, organize, direct, and participate in treatment programs and activities to facilitate patients' rehabilitation, help them integrate into the community, and prevent further medical problems.](Tasks/395.mdx)
- [Observe, analyze, and record patients' participation, reactions, and progress during treatment sessions, modifying treatment programs as needed.](Tasks/387.mdx)
- [Develop treatment plan to meet needs of patient, based on needs assessment, patient interests, and objectives of therapy.](Tasks/388.mdx)
- [Obtain information from medical records, medical staff, family members and the patients, themselves, to assess patients' capabilities, needs and interests.](Tasks/394.mdx)
- [Confer with members of treatment team to plan and evaluate therapy programs.](Tasks/391.mdx)
- [Counsel and encourage patients to develop leisure activities.](Tasks/390.mdx)
- [Encourage clients with special needs and circumstances to acquire new skills and get involved in health-promoting leisure activities, such as sports, games, arts and crafts, and gardening.](Tasks/389.mdx)
- [Prepare and submit reports and charts to treatment team to reflect patients' reactions and evidence of progress or regression.](Tasks/396.mdx)

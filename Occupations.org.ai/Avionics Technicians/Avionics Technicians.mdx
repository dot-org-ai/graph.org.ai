# Avionics Technicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 49-2091.00

Install, inspect, test, adjust, or repair avionics equipment, such as radar, radio, navigation, and missile control systems in aircraft or space vehicles.

## Tasks

- [Test and troubleshoot instruments, components, and assemblies, using circuit testers, oscilloscopes, or voltmeters.](Tasks/9934.mdx)
- [Keep records of maintenance and repair work.](Tasks/9935.mdx)
- [Adjust, repair, or replace malfunctioning components or assemblies, using hand tools or soldering irons.](Tasks/9939.mdx)
- [Install electrical and electronic components, assemblies, and systems in aircraft, using hand tools, power tools, or soldering irons.](Tasks/9938.mdx)
- [Set up and operate ground support and test equipment to perform functional flight tests of electrical and electronic systems.](Tasks/9933.mdx)
- [Assemble components such as switches, electrical controls, and junction boxes, using hand tools or soldering irons.](Tasks/9941.mdx)
- [Lay out installation of aircraft assemblies and systems, following documentation such as blueprints, manuals, and wiring diagrams.](Tasks/9943.mdx)
- [Connect components to assemblies such as radio systems, instruments, magnetos, inverters, and in-flight refueling systems, using hand tools and soldering irons.](Tasks/9940.mdx)
- [Interpret flight test data to diagnose malfunctions and systemic performance problems.](Tasks/9937.mdx)
- [Coordinate work with that of engineers, technicians, and other aircraft maintenance personnel.](Tasks/9936.mdx)

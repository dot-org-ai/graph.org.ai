# Substance Abuse and Behavioral Disorder Counselors

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 21-1011.00

Counsel and advise individuals with alcohol, tobacco, drug, or other problems, such as gambling and eating disorders. May counsel individuals, families, or groups or engage in prevention programs.

## Tasks

- [Complete and maintain accurate records or reports regarding the patients' histories and progress, services provided, or other required information.](Tasks/5584.mdx)
- [Counsel clients or patients, individually or in group sessions, to assist in overcoming dependencies, adjusting to life, or making changes.](Tasks/5583.mdx)
- [Assess individuals' degree of drug dependency by collecting and analyzing urine samples.](Tasks/18622.mdx)
- [Follow progress of discharged patients to determine effectiveness of treatments.](Tasks/5601.mdx)
- [Conduct chemical dependency program orientation sessions.](Tasks/5594.mdx)
- [Review and evaluate clients' progress in relation to measurable goals described in treatment and care plans.](Tasks/5586.mdx)
- [Coordinate activities with courts, probation officers, community services, or other post-treatment agencies.](Tasks/5598.mdx)
- [Develop client treatment plans based on research, clinical experience, and client histories.](Tasks/5585.mdx)
- [Modify treatment plans to comply with changes in client status.](Tasks/5590.mdx)
- [Coordinate counseling efforts with mental health professionals or other health professionals, such as doctors, nurses, or social workers.](Tasks/5591.mdx)

# Ophthalmologists, Except Pediatric

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1241.00

Diagnose and perform surgery to treat and help prevent disorders and diseases of the eye. May also provide vision services for treatment including glasses and contacts.

## Tasks

- [Perform comprehensive examinations of the visual system to determine the nature or extent of ocular disorders.](Tasks/17175.mdx)
- [Diagnose or treat injuries, disorders, or diseases of the eye and eye structures including the cornea, sclera, conjunctiva, or eyelids.](Tasks/17176.mdx)
- [Provide or direct the provision of postoperative care.](Tasks/17166.mdx)
- [Develop or implement plans and procedures for ophthalmologic services.](Tasks/17162.mdx)
- [Prescribe or administer topical or systemic medications to treat ophthalmic conditions and to manage pain.](Tasks/17169.mdx)
- [Develop treatment plans based on patients' histories and goals, the nature and severity of disorders, and treatment risks and benefits.](Tasks/17171.mdx)
- [Perform ophthalmic surgeries such as cataract, glaucoma, refractive, corneal, vitro-retinal, eye muscle, or oculoplastic surgeries.](Tasks/17173.mdx)
- [Educate patients about maintenance and promotion of healthy vision.](Tasks/17163.mdx)
- [Document or evaluate patients' medical histories.](Tasks/17167.mdx)
- [Perform, order, or interpret the results of diagnostic or clinical tests.](Tasks/17170.mdx)

# Physicians, Pathologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1222.00

Diagnose diseases and conduct lab tests using organs, body tissues, and fluids. Includes medical examiners.

## Tasks

- [Examine microscopic samples to identify diseases or other abnormalities.](Tasks/17194.mdx)
- [Diagnose diseases or study medical conditions, using techniques such as gross pathology, histology, cytology, cytopathology, clinical chemistry, immunology, flow cytometry, or molecular biology.](Tasks/17195.mdx)
- [Write pathology reports summarizing analyses, results, and conclusions.](Tasks/17190.mdx)
- [Communicate pathologic findings to surgeons or other physicians.](Tasks/17192.mdx)
- [Identify the etiology, pathogenesis, morphological change, and clinical significance of diseases.](Tasks/17187.mdx)
- [Read current literature, talk with colleagues, or participate in professional organizations or conferences to keep abreast of developments in pathology.](Tasks/17180.mdx)
- [Consult with physicians about ordering and interpreting tests or providing treatments.](Tasks/17193.mdx)
- [Analyze and interpret results from tests, such as microbial or parasite tests, urine analyses, hormonal assays, fine needle aspirations (FNAs), and polymerase chain reactions (PCRs).](Tasks/17191.mdx)
- [Review cases by analyzing autopsies, laboratory findings, or case investigation reports.](Tasks/17178.mdx)
- [Manage medical laboratories.](Tasks/17179.mdx)

# Health Information Technologists and Medical Registrars

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-9021.00

Apply knowledge of healthcare and information systems to assist in the design, development, and continued modification and analysis of computerized healthcare systems. Abstract, collect, and analyze treatment and followup information of patients. May educate staff and assist in problem solving to promote the implementation of the healthcare information system. May design, develop, test, and implement databases with complete history, diagnosis, treatment, and health status to help monitor diseases.

## Tasks

- [Assign the patient to diagnosis-related groups (DRGs), using appropriate computer software.](Tasks/22894.mdx)
- [Compile medical care and census data for statistical reports on diseases treated, surgery performed, or use of hospital beds.](Tasks/22895.mdx)
- [Design databases to support healthcare applications, ensuring security, performance and reliability.](Tasks/22896.mdx)
- [Develop in-service educational materials.](Tasks/22897.mdx)
- [Evaluate and recommend upgrades or improvements to existing computerized healthcare systems.](Tasks/22898.mdx)
- [Facilitate and promote activities, such as lunches, seminars, or tours, to foster healthcare information privacy or security awareness within the organization.](Tasks/22899.mdx)
- [Identify, compile, abstract, and code patient data, using standard classification systems.](Tasks/22900.mdx)
- [Manage the department or supervise clerical workers, directing or controlling activities of personnel in the medical records department.](Tasks/22901.mdx)
- [Monitor changes in legislation and accreditation standards that affect information security or privacy in the computerized healthcare system.](Tasks/22902.mdx)
- [Plan, develop, maintain, or operate a variety of health record indexes or storage and retrieval systems to collect, classify, store, or analyze information.](Tasks/22903.mdx)

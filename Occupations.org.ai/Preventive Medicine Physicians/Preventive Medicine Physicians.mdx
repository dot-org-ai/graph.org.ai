# Preventive Medicine Physicians

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-1229.05

Apply knowledge of general preventive medicine and public health issues to promote health care to groups or individuals, and aid in the prevention or reduction of risk of disease, injury, disability, or death. May practice population-based medicine or diagnose and treat patients in the context of clinical health promotion and disease prevention.

## Tasks

- [Direct or manage prevention programs in specialty areas such as aerospace, occupational, infectious disease, and environmental medicine.](Tasks/17222.mdx)
- [Document or review comprehensive patients' histories with an emphasis on occupation or environmental risks.](Tasks/17212.mdx)
- [Identify groups at risk for specific preventable diseases or injuries.](Tasks/17217.mdx)
- [Perform epidemiological investigations of acute and chronic diseases.](Tasks/17220.mdx)
- [Supervise or coordinate the work of physicians, nurses, statisticians, or other professional staff members.](Tasks/17214.mdx)
- [Design or use surveillance tools, such as screening, lab reports, and vital records, to identify health risks.](Tasks/17218.mdx)
- [Direct public health education programs dealing with topics such as preventable diseases, injuries, nutrition, food service sanitation, water supply safety, sewage and waste disposal, insect control, and immunizations.](Tasks/17219.mdx)
- [Evaluate the effectiveness of prescribed risk reduction measures or other interventions.](Tasks/17216.mdx)
- [Provide information about potential health hazards and possible interventions to the media, the public, other health care professionals, or local, state, and federal health authorities.](Tasks/17225.mdx)
- [Teach or train medical staff regarding preventive medicine issues.](Tasks/17211.mdx)

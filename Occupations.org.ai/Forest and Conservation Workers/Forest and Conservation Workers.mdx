# Forest and Conservation Workers

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 45-4011.00

Under supervision, perform manual labor necessary to develop, maintain, or protect areas such as forests, forested areas, woodlands, wetlands, and rangelands through such activities as raising and transporting seedlings; combating insects, pests, and diseases harmful to plant life; and building structures to control water, erosion, and leaching of soil. Includes forester aides, seedling pullers, tree planters, and gatherers of nontimber forestry products such as pine straw.

## Tasks

- [Check equipment to ensure that it is operating properly.](Tasks/7026.mdx)
- [Fight forest fires or perform prescribed burning tasks under the direction of fire suppression officers or forestry technicians.](Tasks/7028.mdx)
- [Perform fire protection or suppression duties, such as constructing fire breaks or disposing of brush.](Tasks/7029.mdx)
- [Confer with other workers to discuss issues, such as safety, cutting heights, or work needs.](Tasks/7027.mdx)
- [Maintain tallies of trees examined and counted during tree marking or measuring efforts.](Tasks/7035.mdx)
- [Explain or enforce regulations regarding camping, vehicle use, fires, use of buildings, or sanitation.](Tasks/7041.mdx)
- [Operate skidders, bulldozers, or other prime movers to pull a variety of scarification or site preparation equipment over areas to be regenerated.](Tasks/7042.mdx)
- [Spray or inject vegetation with insecticides to kill insects or to protect against disease or with herbicides to reduce competing vegetation.](Tasks/7032.mdx)
- [Thin or space trees, using power thinning saws.](Tasks/7034.mdx)
- [Identify diseased or undesirable trees and remove them, using power saws or hand saws.](Tasks/7031.mdx)

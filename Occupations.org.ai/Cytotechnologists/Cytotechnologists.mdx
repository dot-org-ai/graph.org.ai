# Cytotechnologists

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 29-2011.02

Stain, mount, and study cells to detect evidence of cancer, hormonal abnormalities, and other pathological conditions following established standards and practices.

## Tasks

- [Examine cell samples to detect abnormalities in the color, shape, or size of cellular components and patterns.](Tasks/17395.mdx)
- [Document specimens by verifying patients' and specimens' information.](Tasks/17401.mdx)
- [Submit slides with abnormal cell structures to pathologists for further examination.](Tasks/17405.mdx)
- [Prepare and analyze samples, such as Papanicolaou (PAP) smear body fluids and fine needle aspirations (FNAs), to detect abnormal conditions.](Tasks/17397.mdx)
- [Examine specimens, using microscopes, to evaluate specimen quality.](Tasks/17396.mdx)
- [Maintain effective laboratory operations by adhering to standards of specimen collection, preparation, or laboratory safety.](Tasks/17402.mdx)
- [Provide patient clinical data or microscopic findings to assist pathologists in the preparation of pathology reports.](Tasks/17398.mdx)
- [Assist pathologists or other physicians to collect cell samples by fine needle aspiration (FNA) biopsy or other method.](Tasks/17399.mdx)
- [Prepare cell samples by applying special staining techniques, such as chromosomal staining, to differentiate cells or cell components.](Tasks/17404.mdx)
- [Adjust, maintain, or repair laboratory equipment, such as microscopes.](Tasks/17406.mdx)

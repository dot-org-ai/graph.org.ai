# Layout Workers, Metal and Plastic

**O*NET-SOC Code**: 51-4192.00

Lay out reference points and dimensions on metal or plastic stock or workpieces, such as sheets, plates, tubes, structural shapes, castings, or machine parts, for further processing. Includes shipfitters.

## Tasks

- [Mark curves, lines, holes, dimensions, and welding symbols onto workpieces, using scribes, soapstones, punches, and hand drills.](Tasks/10298.mdx)
- [Plan locations and sequences of cutting, drilling, bending, rolling, punching, and welding operations, using compasses, protractors, dividers, and rules.](Tasks/10302.mdx)
- [Fit and align fabricated parts to be welded or assembled.](Tasks/10295.mdx)
- [Locate center lines and verify template positions, using measuring instruments such as gauge blocks, height gauges, and dial indicators.](Tasks/10300.mdx)
- [Plan and develop layouts from blueprints and templates, applying knowledge of trigonometry, design, effects of heat, and properties of metals.](Tasks/10296.mdx)
- [Lay out and fabricate metal structural parts such as plates, bulkheads, and frames.](Tasks/10297.mdx)
- [Compute layout dimensions, and determine and mark reference points on metal stock or workpieces for further processing, such as welding and assembly.](Tasks/10299.mdx)
- [Lift and position workpieces in relation to surface plates, manually or with hoists, and using parallel blocks and angle plates.](Tasks/10301.mdx)
- [Design and prepare templates of wood, paper, or metal.](Tasks/10304.mdx)
- [Install doors, hatches, brackets, and clips.](Tasks/10307.mdx)
